### Question 5 — Analyses
*Specify exactly which analyses you will conduct to examine the main question/hypothesis.*

> Map to Section 8 of `preregistration_template.md`.  
> Include: statistical test, outcome variable, predictor(s), covariates, software/package, alpha level, and any multiple-comparison correction.  
> Every analysis listed here is **confirmatory**. Any analysis not listed is **exploratory**.  
> Example: *"Multiple linear regression predicting mean pro-conservation intentions (Q3) from place identity (continuous), controlling for age, gender, and urban/rural status. R, lm(). α = .05, no correction (single primary test). Standardized β will be reported."*

### Question 6 — Outliers and exclusions
*Describe exactly how outliers will be defined and handled, and your precise rule(s) for excluding observations.*

> Map to Section 7 of `preregistration_template.md`.  
> Be operationally precise — avoid vague language like "poor quality responses."  
> Specify: attention check rules, completion time thresholds, response pattern checks (e.g., straight-lining), missing data rules, and outlier winsorizing/trimming rules.  
> Example: *"Exclusion: (1) < 2 of 3 attention checks correct; (2) survey completed in < 90 seconds; (3) > 20% item non-response on any scale. Outliers: values > 3 SD from the scale mean will be winsorized to 3 SD."*

**Answer:**

---

### Question 7 — Sample size
*How many observations will be collected or what will determine sample size?*

> Map to Section 6 of `preregistration_template.md` and `Step_09_Power_and_Sampling/`.  
> State the target N, how it was determined (power analysis parameters), and the stopping rule.  
> Example: *"Target N = 350 complete responses, based on a power analysis for multiple regression (f² = 0.10, α = .05, power = .90, 6 predictors; G*Power 3.1). Data collection ends when N = 350 or on [date], whichever comes first."*
