| Theory / Framework | Main Argument | Primary Author(s) & Year |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Value-Belief-Norm** | arguably the most cited theory in environmental behavior research; links ecological worldviews → beliefs → moral norms → action | VBN) Theory (Stern et al., 1999 |
| **Norm Activation Model** | the foundation VBN builds on; models altruistic/moral norm activation | NAM) (Schwartz, 1977 |
| **New Ecological Paradigm** | foundational instrument for measuring anthropocentric vs. ecocentric worldviews | NEP) (Dunlap & Van Liere, 1978 |
| **Nature Connectedness / Connectedness to Nature** | emotional and cognitive bonds to the natural world as a behavioral predictor | Nisbet et al., 2009; Schultz, 2002 |
| **Common-Pool Resource** | the cornerstone of socio-ecological systems science; explains when communities self-govern shared resources | CPR) Theory (Ostrom, 1990 |
| **Collective Action Theory** | logic of free-riding, a fundamental tension in conservation | Olson, 1965 |
| **Institutional Analysis and Development** | analytical framework for studying rule systems governing social-ecological interactions | IAD) Framework (Ostrom, 2005 |
| **Social-Ecological Systems** | diagnostic framework for sustainability governance | SES) Framework (Ostrom, 2009 |
| **Resilience Theory / Adaptive Cycle** | how SES persist, adapt, or transform through disturbance | Holling, 1973; Folke et al., 2004 |
| **Panarchy Theory** | nested adaptive cycles across scales; explains cross-scale dynamics in SES | Gunderson & Holling, 2002 |
| **Complex Adaptive Systems** | emergence, self-organization, and non-linearity in socio-ecological dynamics | CAS |
| **Transtheoretical Model / Stages of Change** | maps readiness for behavior change across discrete stages | Prochaska & DiClemente, 1983 |
| **COM-B Model / Behavior Change Wheel** | systematic framework decomposing behavior into Capability, Opportunity, Motivation | Michie et al., 2011 |
| **Diffusion of Innovations** | how new conservation practices spread through social systems | Rogers, 1962 |
| **Multi-Level Perspective** | how niches, regimes, and landscapes interact in sustainability transitions | MLP) (Geels, 2002 |
| **Transformative Learning Theory** | how disorienting dilemmas shift deep worldview assumptions | Mezirow, 1991 |
| **Prospect Theory** | loss aversion and framing effects; critical for conservation policy design | Kahneman & Tversky, 1979 |
| **Protection Motivation Theory** | threat appraisal and coping appraisal as behavioral drivers | Rogers, 1975 |
| **Social Norms Theory** | descriptive vs. injunctive norms; highly relevant for community-based conservation | Cialdini et al., 1990 |
| **Environmental Identity Theory** | how self-concept includes nature-related roles | Clayton, 2003 |
| **Place Attachment Theory** | person-place bonds as drivers of stewardship behavior | Scannell & Gifford, 2010 |
| **Moral Foundations Theory** | how different moral intuitions shape responses to conservation messages | Haidt, 2012 |
| **Social Practice Theory** | habits and routines as the unit of analysis rather than individual decisions | Shove, Pantzar & Watson, 2012 |
| **Structuration Theory** | the mutual constitution of social structure and human agency | Giddens, 1984 |
