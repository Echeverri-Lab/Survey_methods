# Survey Design Matrix — Echeverri et al. (2019)
## *Avian Cultural Ecosystem Services: A Framework and Pilot Survey*

> **How to read this matrix.**  
> Every row represents one survey *block* or item. Columns capture: the research question it addresses, the theoretical construct it operationalises, its variable role in the analysis, the scale or source it draws from, the response format, and placement/order notes. Items with no analytic justification were removed before piloting — this matrix enforces that discipline.

| Field | Detail |

### Page 3 — Open-ended Birds (Qualitative data to supplement)

| Block ID | Item / Block | Research Question Addressed | Theoretical Construct | Variable Role | Scale / Source | Response Format | Order Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P3-01 | "What do you value most about birds in this area?" (positive) | RQ1: What CES do people attribute to birds? | Cultural ES — positive | Qualitative supplement | Custom open-ended | Free text | Before species loop |
| P3-02 | "Are there things you want future generations to be able to experience with birds here?" (bequest) | RQ1: Bequest value | Cultural ES — bequest | Qualitative supplement | Custom open-ended | Free text | After positive |

> **Note.** Open-ended items are placed *before* the structured Likert blocks to avoid anchoring qualitative responses to the scale language. Responses were analysed with thematic coding (Braun & Clarke, 2006).

### Pages 4–16 — Bird-specific Loop × 12 Species (Dependent variables + Qualitative)

*Each of the 12 focal bird species received its own page-set. The loop below repeats for every species.*

#### Sub-loop per species (Pages 4 through 16, ×12)

| Block ID | Item / Block | Research Question Addressed | Theoretical Construct | Variable Role | Scale / Source | Response Format | Order Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SP-01 | Photograph + audio playback of species | — | Stimulus presentation | Not in analyses | Species media library (Macaulay Library) | Passive viewing / listening | Always first in loop |
| SP-02 | **Familiarity**: "How familiar are you with this bird?" | RQ3: Does familiarity moderate values? | Species familiarity | **Random effect** | 5-point Likert (1 = Not at all, 5 = Extremely) | Slider / radio | Immediately after stimulus |
| SP-03 | **Like/dislike**: "Overall, how much do you like or dislike this bird?" | RQ2: Affective valence | Affective attitude | **Dependent variable** | 7-point bipolar scale (−3 = Strongly dislike, +3 = Strongly like) | Slider | After familiarity |
| SP-04 | Open-ended name: "What is this bird called in your community?" | RQ3: Local ecological knowledge | TEK / local name | Qualitative supplement | Custom open-ended | Free text | After like/dislike |
| SP-05 | Open-ended why: "Why do you like or dislike this bird?" | RQ1: Mechanisms behind affect | CES mechanisms | Qualitative supplement | Custom open-ended | Free text | After local name |
| SP-06 | **Identity (+)**: "This bird makes me feel proud of where I live." | RQ1: Identity value | CES — place identity (positive) | **Dependent variable (1)** | Avian CES Scale (Echeverri et al. 2019) | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-07 | **Recreation/aesthetic (+)** ×3 | RQ1: Recreation & aesthetic value | CES — recreation; CES — aesthetic | **Dependent variables (3)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-08 | **Labor farmer (−)**: "This bird damages my crops." | RQ2: Negative agri. interaction | CES disservice — farmer labor | **Dependent variable (1)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-09 | **Labor farmer (+)**: "This bird helps control pests on my farm." | RQ2: Positive agri. service | CES service — farmer labor | **Dependent variable (1)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-10 | **Identity (−)**: "This bird makes me feel unsafe or uncomfortable." | RQ1: Negative identity | CES disservice — identity (negative) | **Dependent variable (1)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-11 | **Labor scientific (+)** ×2 | RQ1: Scientific / educational value | CES — knowledge; CES — education | **Dependent variables (2)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |
| SP-12 | **Bequest (+)** ×2 | RQ1: Bequest / existence value | CES — bequest | **Dependent variables (2)** | Avian CES Scale | 5-point Likert | Fixed block order |

> **Randomization note.** The order of the 12 species was randomized across participants to control for fatigue and ordering effects. The order of CES subscale items within each loop was fixed (not randomized) to mirror validated scale administration procedures.

> **Analytic structure note.** The repeated-measures design (12 species per respondent) was analysed with mixed-effects models. Species and participant were treated as crossed random effects. Familiarity (SP-02) entered as a species-level random effect covariate.

### Page 18 — Demographics (Independent variables / covariates)

| Block ID | Item / Block | Research Question Addressed | Theoretical Construct | Variable Role | Scale / Source | Response Format | Order Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P18-01 | Age | RQ3: Demographic moderators | Age | **Covariate** | Custom | Open numeric or range | End of survey |
| P18-02 | Gender | RQ3: Demographic moderators | Gender identity | **Covariate** | Custom inclusive categories | Categorical (incl. non-binary, prefer not to say) | End of survey |
| P18-03 | Home: "Have you always lived in this community?" | RQ3: Community embeddedness | Place attachment proxy | **Covariate** | Custom | Yes / No + years | End of survey |
| P18-04 | Education | RQ3: Demographic moderators | Educational attainment | **Covariate** | Standard census categories | Categorical | End of survey |
| P18-05 | Occupation | RQ3: Demographic moderators | Livelihood type | **Covariate** | Custom (farmer / tourism / other) | Categorical | End of survey |

> **Placement rationale.** Demographics are placed at the *end* of the survey following best practice (Danaher & Crandall, 2008). Asking about gender, income, or ethnicity at the beginning can trigger social identity threat and bias responses to subsequent attitude items (Steele & Aronson, 1995).

## Section D — Respondent Burden Audit

| Survey component | Estimated time | Cumulative |
|---|---|---|
| Consent + intro (P1) | 2 min | 2 min |
| Self-identification (P2) | 1 min | 3 min |
| General open-ended questions (P3) | 3 min | 6 min |
| 12 × bird loop (P4–16) | ~1.5 min × 12 = 18 min | 24 min |
| Birding experience (P17) | 2 min | 26 min |
| Demographics (P18) | 3 min | 29 min |
| Conclusion (P19) | 1 min | 30 min |
| **Total** | **~30 min** | |

> **Rule of thumb.** Online surveys: aim for ≤ 15 min. Face-to-face with enumerator and engaging multimedia stimuli: ≤ 35 min is acceptable. Beyond 40 min, dropout and satisficing increase markedly (Galesic & Bosnjak, 2009).
