# Survey Question Justification Matrix — FFCP Pre-Monitoring Survey

**Study:** Family Forest Carbon Program (FFCP) — Wildlife Monitoring Add-On  
**Survey timing:** Pre-monitoring (repeated-measures design; paired with a post-monitoring follow-up survey)  
**Partner organization:** Appalachian Mountain Joint Venture (AMJV)  
**Source:** Kelley (unpublished; shared with Echeverri Lab)

---

## Background

This matrix documents every question included in the pre-monitoring survey administered to forest landowners enrolled in the Family Forest Carbon Program who agreed to participate in a wildlife monitoring add-on study led by the AMJV. Each item is justified against a specific research purpose, and the construction rationale (existing scale, adapted item, or custom item) is noted.

---

## Question Justification Matrix

| # | Survey Question | Purpose / Research Justification | Construction Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *If you agree to the above and are 18 years or older, please indicate below and click the arrow to proceed to the survey. If you do not agree to the above, please indicate that below.* | Necessary consent question required by IRB. Branching logic: selecting "I do not agree" triggers opt-out end-of-survey message. | IRB/BREB standard practice — custom wording |
| 2 | *How satisfied are you with your experience participating in the Family Forest Carbon Program overall?* | Repeated-measures item (pre → post). Gauges whether participation in wildlife monitoring affects overall satisfaction with FFCP. | Custom item; single-item satisfaction measure |
| 3 | *How much do you trust information related to forest management practices provided to you through the Family Forest Carbon Program?* | Repeated-measures item (pre → post). Gauges whether participation in wildlife monitoring affects trust in FFCP-provided information. | Custom item; single-item trust measure |
| 4 | *Please rate how important each of the following is to your decision to participate in the Family Forest Carbon Program.* | Repeated-measures item (pre → post). Tracks how motivations for FFCP participation shift with wildlife monitoring exposure — specifically whether habitat protection and interacting with experts become more salient. Items selected from Holland et al. (unpublished) survey findings plus key program elements (combating climate change, financial incentives, expert interaction). | Adapted from Holland et al. (unpublished); custom additions |
| 5 | *How important are the following as reasons for why you own the wooded land you have enrolled in the Family Forest Carbon Program?* | Benchmarking item. Allows comparison of respondents to a national sample of family forest owners and exploration of how land-ownership values relate to program experience. | Direct copy from the **National Woodland Owners Survey (NWOS)** |
| 6 | *Why did you agree to participate in the wildlife monitoring performed by the Appalachian Mountain Joint Venture?* | Assesses motivations for program enrollment; results can inform future recruitment strategies. Items can also be revisited in the post-survey to evaluate whether each objective was achieved. | Custom item |
| 7 | *Please share your name.* | Necessary for linking pre-survey responses to post-survey responses in a repeated-measures design. | Administrative / study-design necessity |
| 8 | *Is there anything else you would like to share?* | Open-ended exit item following best practice. Captures unsolicited thoughts and concerns in-survey rather than via email, reducing researcher burden and improving data traceability. | Best-practice open-ended close (Tourangeau et al., 2000) |

---

## Design Notes

- **Repeated-measures items (Q2, Q3, Q4):** Identical wording must be preserved in the post-monitoring survey to enable valid within-subject comparisons.
- **National Woodland Owners Survey item (Q5):** Because this is a verbatim copy, response options and scale anchors must also match the NWOS instrument exactly to preserve comparability.
- **Name collection (Q7):** Consider whether a unique ID code could substitute for names to reduce privacy risk while still enabling record linkage (discuss with IRB).
- **Survey bloat check:** Every item in this matrix has an explicit justification. Items without a clear purpose were excluded before piloting — consistent with the survey matrix approach described in the Step 6 README.
