# Psychometric Scales Reference Table

*Note: The scales listed below vary widely in their scope and application, ranging from **general dispositional traits** (e.g., NRS, EID) to **context-specific attitudes** (e.g., bat scale, Echeverri birds). This variation highlights the importance of careful construct selection to match your theoretical model.*

| **Original NEP** | New Environmental Paradigm Scale | Values & worldview | Pro-ecological worldview assessing beliefs about the "balance of nature," "limits to growth," and "human dominance over nature" | 12 items (8 pro-NEP, 4 anti-NEP); [VERIFY] | Yes (Revised NEP) | [VERIFY] | Dunlap & Van Liere, 1978 | - |
| **Revised NEP** | New Ecological Paradigm Scale | Values & worldview | Worldview or belief system regarding humanity's relationship with the natural environment (taps a wider range of facets, balanced pro- and anti-NEP items, avoids outmoded terminology) | 15 items; 5-point Likert | Yes | Yes | Dunlap et al., 2000 | - |
| **RV** | Relational Values Scale | Values & worldview | Values linking people and ecosystems via tangible and intangible relationships to nature, principles, virtues, and notions of a good life (distinct from NEP and intrinsic/instrumental values) | 6 items; [VERIFY] | No | Yes (US, Costa Rica) | Klain et al., 2017 | - |
| **N/A** | Kellert Typology of Basic Attitudes towards Animals | Attitudes to animals | How individuals value, use, or relate to wildlife and nature across nine distinct categories (naturalistic, ecologistic, humanistic, moralistic, scientific, aesthetic, utilitarian, dominionistic, negativistic) | Multiple items per dimension; structured interview / survey | No | Partial | Kellert, 1984 | - |
| **EID** | Environmental Identity Scale | Identity | Cognitive and emotional components of one's relationship with nature (Salience of Identity, Feelings of Membership, Acceptance of Ideology, Positive Emotions, Autobiographical Component) | 24 items; 7-point Likert | Yes (EID-R; Clayton et al., 2021) | No / Partial | Clayton, 2003 | - |
| **EID-R** | Revised Environmental Identity Scale | Identity | Cross-culturally validated revision of the EID; examines environmental identity | 14 items; 7-point Likert | N/A | Yes | Clayton et al., 2021 | 11 original EID items modified + 3 new items ("steward of natural resources", "comfortable out in nature", "enjoy encountering nature in city setting") |
| **NRS** | Nature Relatedness Scale (NRS-21) | Nature connectedness | Subjective sense of connection with nature (cognitive, affective, and experiential), evaluating a stable trait of interconnectedness across three factors: NR-Self, NR-Perspective, and NR-Experience | 21 items; 5-point Likert | Yes (NR-6; Nisbet & Zelenski, 2013) | Yes | Nisbet et al., 2009 | - |
| **NR-6** | Nature Relatedness Short Form | Nature connectedness | Concise measure for efficient assessment of nature relatedness, retaining high reliability and focusing on the 'Self' and 'Experience' dimensions of the original scale | 6 items; 5-point Likert | N/A | Yes | Nisbet & Zelenski, 2013 | - |
| **VO** | Wildlife Value Orientations | Values & worldview | Mutualism vs. domination orientations towards wildlife (appropriate use, hunting, social affiliation, caring); classifies individuals into Mutualists, Traditionalists, Pluralists, or Distanced | 19 items; 7-point Likert | Partial | Yes | Teel & Manfredo, 2010 | - |
| **AAS** | Animal Attitude Scale | Attitudes to animals | Attitudes toward the ethics of the use of other species and general animal welfare | 20 items; 5-point Likert | Yes (AAS-5, AAS-10; Herzog et al., 2015) | Yes | Herzog et al., 1991 | - |
| **AAS short forms** | Animal Attitude Scale - Short Forms (AAS-5, AAS-10) | Attitudes to animals | Brief measures of attitudes related to animal welfare (highly correlated with the original AAS) | 5 or 10 items; 5-point Likert | N/A | Yes | Herzog et al., 2015 | - |
| **PANAS** | Positive and Negative Affect Schedule | Affect | Two distinct dimensions of affect (Positive Affect / Negative Affect); tracks emotional changes "at the moment" or over a specific timeframe | 20 items; 5-point Likert | Yes (I-PANAS-SF; Thompson, 2007) | Yes | Watson et al., 1988 | - |
| **I-PANAS-SF** | International Positive and Negative Affect Schedule Short Form | Affect | Brief measure of Positive and Negative Affect designed to reduce cross-cultural linguistic ambiguity | 10 items; 5-point Likert | N/A | Yes | Thompson, 2007 | - |
| **SPANE** | Scale of Positive and Negative Experience | Affect | Frequency of emotional experiences (Positive, Negative, and Affect Balance) based on broad feeling descriptors | 12 items; 5-point Likert | No | Yes | Diener et al., 2010 | - |
| **POMS** | Profile of Mood States | Affect | Transient or enduring mood states across six dimensions (Tension-Anxiety, Depression-Dejection, Anger-Hostility, Vigor-Activity, Fatigue-Inertia, Confusion-Bewilderment) and Total Mood Disturbance | 65 items; [VERIFY] | [VERIFY] | [VERIFY] | McNair et al., 1971 | - |
| **STAI** | State-Trait Anxiety Inventory | Affect | Differentiates between temporary "state" anxiety (current feelings of tension) and long-standing "trait" anxiety (stable propensity to perceive situations as threatening) | 40 items; [VERIFY] | [VERIFY] | [VERIFY] | Spielberger, 1983 | - |
| **WEMWBS** | Warwick–Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale | Wellbeing | Positive aspects of mental health including hedonic (happiness), eudaimonic (psychological functioning/sense of purpose), and social wellbeing | 14 items; 5-point Likert | Yes (SWEMWBS - 7 items) | Yes | Tennant et al., 2007 | - |
| **BAtSS** | Bats Attitudes Standard Scale | Attitudes to animals | Attitudes towards bats to aid conservation, assessing four key factors (scientistic, positivistic, negativistic, myths) along with emotional, behavioral, and cognitive subfactors | 34 items; 5-point Likert | No | Partial (Developed in Chile) | Pérez et al., 2021 | Taxa explicitly adapted for bats |
| **Echeverri birds** | Echeverri Bird Attitudes Scale | Attitudes to animals | Attitudes towards birds and their associated cultural ecosystem services and disservices (Disservices, Education, Bequest, Birdwatching, Acoustic Aesthetic, Identity) | 14 items; Likert scale | No | No | Echeverri et al., 2019 | Transcends contexts but relies on distinct avian cultural ecosystem services |
| **Geladi birds** | Geladi Avian Ecosystem (Dis)service Scale | Attitudes to animals | Avian ecosystem services and disservices among farmers, combining open-ended qualitative codes with corresponding Likert statements on benefits and negative effects | Mixed qualitative and 7-point Likert scale | No | No | Geladi et al., 2025 | Taxa explicitly adapted for birds |
| **PAS** | Plant Attitude Scale | Attitudes to plants | Attitudes toward plants across four distinct dimensions (Interest, Importance, Urban trees, and Utilization) | 29 items; Likert-scale | No | Partial (Validated in Slovakia) | Fančovičová & Prokop, 2010 | - |
