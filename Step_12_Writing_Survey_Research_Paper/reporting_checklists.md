# Reporting Checklists for Survey Research

Use the appropriate checklist(s) below when writing up your study. Complete each item before submission and include as a supplementary file where journals require it.

---

## A. CHERRIES — Checklist for Reporting Results of Internet E-Surveys
*(Eysenbach, 2004)*

### Design
- [ ] Was the study design described (e.g., cross-sectional, longitudinal)?
- [ ] Was the target population defined?
- [ ] Was an a priori sample size justification provided?
- [ ] Was the survey development and content validity described?

### Participants
- [ ] Was survey open to all or targeted?
- [ ] Were inclusion/exclusion criteria stated?
- [ ] Was IRB/ethics board approval reported (name, protocol number, date)?

### Statistics
- [ ] Was the response rate reported using a standard definition?
- [ ] Was a denominator for the response rate specified?
- [ ] Was the completion rate reported (% of starters who finished)?

### Instrument
- [ ] Were all items (or citation to published scale + modifications) reported?
- [ ] Was the order of questions described?
- [ ] Were skip/branching rules described?
- [ ] Was the survey platform named?

### Results
- [ ] Were exclusions and data quality procedures described?
- [ ] Was the recruitment method described?
- [ ] Were study dates (start and end of data collection) reported?

## B. SRQR — Standards for Reporting Qualitative Research
*(O'Brien et al., 2014)*

### Title and Abstract
- [ ] Do the title/abstract indicate qualitative methods?

### Introduction
- [ ] Is the problem/question clearly stated with scholarly context?
- [ ] Is the purpose or research question stated?

### Methods
- [ ] Is the qualitative approach and research paradigm named (e.g., constructivism, interpretivism)?
- [ ] Is the researcher's role and reflexivity addressed?
- [ ] Is the context described (setting, participants, time)?
- [ ] Is sampling strategy described and justified?
- [ ] Is data collection described (instrument, process, number of coders)?
- [ ] Is data processing described (transcription, management)?
- [ ] Is data analysis described (approach: inductive/deductive, software, codebook development)?
- [ ] Is trustworthiness / credibility addressed (member checks, triangulation, peer debriefing)?

### Results
- [ ] Are findings synthesized coherently with themes/categories?
- [ ] Are representative quotes included and appropriately attributed?
- [ ] Is disconfirming evidence addressed?

### Discussion
- [ ] Are findings interpreted in relation to prior work?
- [ ] Are implications for practice or policy stated?
- [ ] Are transferability and limitations discussed?

## C. COREQ — Consolidated Criteria for Reporting Qualitative Research
*(Tong et al., 2007)*

### Research Team and Reflexivity
- [ ] Personal characteristics of interviewer/researcher reported (role, credentials, experience)
- [ ] Relationship with participants described (prior acquaintance, assumptions)

### Study Design
- [ ] Theoretical framework named
- [ ] Participant selection strategy described (purposive, snowball, convenience)
- [ ] Method of approach described (in person, phone, email)
- [ ] Sample size and reason for stopping sampling stated
- [ ] Non-participation rate and reasons reported
- [ ] Setting of data collection described

### Analysis and Findings
- [ ] Number of coders reported
- [ ] Description of coding tree / codebook included
- [ ] Derivation of themes described (data-driven vs. a priori)
- [ ] Software used reported
- [ ] Participant checking (member checks) addressed
- [ ] Quotations attributed by pseudonym/ID

## D. APA JARS-Quant — Journal Article Reporting Standards (Quantitative)
*(Levitt et al., 2018)*

### Method Section Requirements
- [ ] Participants: total N, recruitment method, eligibility criteria, compensation, demographics
- [ ] Measures: source/citation for each scale, all modifications listed verbatim, evidence of reliability and validity
- [ ] Research design: between/within subjects, experimental vs. observational, randomization
- [ ] Procedure: data collection timeline, mode, platform
- [ ] Pre-registration: registry, date, link, or rationale for absence
- [ ] Missing data: approach described and justified

### Results Section Requirements
- [ ] Sample description table
- [ ] Reliability coefficients with confidence intervals for all scales
- [ ] Effect sizes with confidence intervals for all comparisons
- [ ] Exact test statistics and degrees of freedom
- [ ] Figures and tables conform to APA 7th edition formatting

---

## E. APA JARS-Qual — Journal Article Reporting Standards (Qualitative)
*(Levitt et al., 2018)*

### Method Section Requirements
- [ ] Research tradition named (phenomenology, grounded theory, thematic analysis, etc.)
- [ ] Researcher positionality statement included
- [ ] Trustworthiness strategies described
- [ ] Data saturation or saturation rationale described
- [ ] Coding approach (inductive/deductive/hybrid) named
- [ ] Inter-rater reliability: statistic, value, and subset described
- [ ] Codebook included as supplementary material

### Results Section Requirements
- [ ] Themes/codes presented with frequency counts if relevant
- [ ] Illustrative quotes included (attributed, re-identifying details redacted)
- [ ] Negative cases or disconfirming evidence addressed
